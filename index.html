Nikolaos Tziavelis (Nikos)

I am an Assistant Professor in UC Santa Cruz. My research spans both theoretical and practical aspects of database systems. I like working on problems in database theory and leveraging these ideas to improve the performance of real-world systems.

I am actively looking for motivated PhD students to join my group. If you have a strong background in databases/systems, or if you are excited about designing algorithms for problems that come up in data processing systems, apply to the PhD program of UC Santa Cruz and mention my name in your application.

Short Bio

Nikolaos (Nikos) Tziavelis is an Assistant Professor in the CSE Department in Basking Engineering, UC Santa Cruz. His research interests span both theoretical and practical aspects of data systems, leveraging ideas from database theory to design innovative solutions for real-world systems. He received his Ph.D. from Northeastern University advised by Mirek Riedewald and Wolfgang Gatterbauer. Before that, he received a Diploma from the National Technical University of Athens, Greece. His work has been awarded with a 2022 Google PhD fellowship, a PODS 2021 “Best of” recognition, a 2023 VLDB PhD Workshop Best Paper Award, and the 2024 Khoury Research Award from Northeastern University.
